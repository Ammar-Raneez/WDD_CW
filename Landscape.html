    • Why Landscape ?

    Landscape Photography is a term that refers to one of two possiblities. On one hand, it deals with photographs that are taken in a Landscape Orientation; i.e.: those where the width is greater than the height. On the otherhand, it refers photographs taken with a focus on the background rather than the foreground. This would be clearly seen in cases like shots of an open plain, a mountain range, a lakefront etc. Landscapes tend to not focus on a single point, rather they focus on the hole image equally, polarising them completely in contrast to Portraits. Some key features of a good Lanscape photograph would be beautifully contrasting colours, & picturesque settings, both natural & artificial. Common techniques involved in Lanscape photography include the Usage of low ISO Levels & the f22 'Story-Telling' Apeture.
